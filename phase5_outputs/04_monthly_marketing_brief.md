### Persona insights

**Gifter (28% — highest volume)**
Gifters are Boldr's most active enquirers in April. They cluster around engraving details: font options, character limits, turnaround time, Chinese/Arabic script, and correction windows. The purchase intent is high — they've already decided to buy — but pre-purchase uncertainty about engraving specifics creates friction. This is a conversion risk.

**Health Conscious (22%)**
Material safety questions span BPA, nickel, titanium grade, EU certifications, silicone grade, and child safety. These buyers do research before purchasing and will not convert without clear answers. Instagram DM is the dominant channel, suggesting they discovered Boldr via social and DM'd rather than using the contact form. Content on product pages is not answering these questions sufficiently.

**Owner Aftercare (17%)**
Existing owners asking about servicing, regulation, case polishing, and international service. This is a loyalty signal — these are customers who've owned the watch long enough to need maintenance. Conversion goal here is not a new sale but retention and lifetime value. The CS engine answers these well from KB.

**Niche Buyer (6% — but high escalation rate)**
Despite low volume, niche_buyer tickets had a 100% knowledge gap rate in April — every one required CS escalation. Questions included watch recommendation for extreme sports and carbon-neutral shipping. These are high-consideration buyers; they're thorough and need thorough answers.

## Section 3 — Product Page Gap Analysis

These are questions customers asked in April that are **not currently answered on the Boldr website** (based on KB gaps flagged during the month). Each represents a content gap that causes unnecessary CS tickets and potential lost conversions.

### Gap 1 — Engraving font options not shown
**Question pattern**: "What font style is used for engraving? Can I see an example?"
**Persona**: gifter
**Tickets**: TKT-1069
**Impact**: High — gifters want to visualise the final product before committing. No font example = uncertainty = abandonment.
**Recommendation**: Add engraving font name + a mockup image to the engraving product page and the engraving FAQ.

### Gap 2 — Child safety / material certifications
**Question pattern**: "Is this watch safe for children? Any certifications?"
**Persona**: health_conscious
**Tickets**: TKT-1066
**Impact**: Medium-High — parents and gift-buyers for children want explicit reassurance. BPA-free and nickel-free facts exist in KB but are not surfaced as a child-safety answer.
**Recommendation**: Add a child safety section to the materials FAQ. If any EU/ASTM certifications apply, surface them explicitly.

### Gap 3 — Carbon-neutral shipping / sustainability
**Question pattern**: "Do you offer carbon-neutral shipping?" / "Is your packaging recyclable?"
**Persona**: niche_buyer, prospect
**Tickets**: TKT-1013, TKT-1024
**Impact**: Medium and rising — sustainability is a growing purchase criterion, particularly for European buyers (Boldr ships internationally). Absence of clear content signals indifference.
**Recommendation**: Add a sustainability page or section. Even if Boldr does not currently offer carbon offset shipping, stating what the brand does do (recyclable packaging, titanium durability as a sustainability argument, etc.) is better than silence.

### Gap 4 — Silicone strap grade (food-grade vs medical-grade)
**Question pattern**: "Is the silicone strap food-grade or medical-grade? My skin is sensitive."
**Persona**: health_conscious
**Tickets**: TKT-1038
**Impact**: Medium — a frequently asked sub-question within material safety. Medical-grade silicone is a selling point if it applies; worth stating clearly on product pages.
**Recommendation**: Confirm strap silicone grade internally, then add to materials FAQ and product listings.

### Gap 5 — Arabic and non-Latin script engraving
**Question pattern**: "Can you engrave in Arabic / Mandarin / [other script]?"
**Persona**: gifter
**Tickets**: TKT-1070, TKT-1049
**Impact**: Medium — the rate card shows Arabic and CJK engraving is supported (SGD 3/char), but this is not visible on the website or engraving FAQ. Customers are enquiring via DM when they should be able to self-serve.
**Recommendation**: Add supported scripts, per-character pricing, and character limits to the engraving product page.
